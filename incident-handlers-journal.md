### The 5 W's

**Who caused the incident?**
An organized group of unethical hackers known for targeting organizations in the healthcare and transportation sectors.

**What happened?**
A ransomware attack was launched against a small U.S. healthcare clinic. The attackers encrypted all critical files and left a ransom note demanding a large sum of money in exchange for the decryption key.

**When did the incident occur?**
Tuesday morning at 9:00 a.m.

**Where did the incident occur?**
At a small U.S.-based primary healthcare clinic. The attack affected the company's internal network and computer systems.

**Why did the incident occur?**
The attackers gained access to the company's network through targeted phishing emails sent to several employees. These emails contained a malicious attachment that, once downloaded, installed malware on the employee's computer. After gaining access, the attackers deployed ransomware that encrypted critical files across the organization's systems.

### Additional Notes
1. How could the healthcare company prevent a similar incident from occurring again? Potential measures include security awareness training for employees, email filtering to detect phishing attempts, and regular data backups.
2. Should the company pay the ransom to retrieve the decryption key? Paying the ransom is generally discouraged by cybersecurity authorities, as it does not guarantee file recovery and encourages further attacks.
